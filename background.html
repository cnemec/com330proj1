My childhood background

My history as a child is fairly normal but at the same time a good basis to understand how I got to where I am today. I had a few brothers one was both athetic and much more socialable then me, which eventually lead to me being the smart one, mostly due to me having mroe time to study. While at first this didn't affect me much eventually I got used to the title and started trying to live up to it. Now this could have led me into any field in stem, maybe an engineer or mathlete, but due to the town I was in not having much to do it led me to playing a lot of video games. Being around something so involved with coding from a young age led me to choosing the field. The classes I took in programming in highschool only made me more sure.

My college choice

At first my college choice was not IIT, like most people at my school I knew nothing about it despite being so close. I was gonna go to UCF, a college in florida I had family there and some programs looked nice. Eventually my dad recommended IIT and the proximity to home and Chicago was tempting but not enough. That all changed when I went to UCF in person, the heat was terrible I couldn't stand it. In ten minutes I felt very sweaty and tired, as such I knew I could never possibly stay there it was too hot for an Illinois boy. Though afterwards I still wasn't sure which chicago university to go to IIT was of course in the lead. But when I went to IIT learned its history and saw the buildings I knew it would be perfect for me. The historic architecture, teh inventor of the cell phone, and the beautiful view of the greatest city on earth. I knew instantly this was the school for me.

My change in perspective

At first my classes were chosen solely based on what I needed to fill on my transcript. The teachers were not important, the classes were just what I needed to learn. This mostly changed recently when I was involved with a car accident. Obviously that wasn't the only part but being outside of class allowed me to see a sudden shift of the students in one. I classes were mostly a place to learn and I took the specifics for granted. I understood how hard classes would be when completely seperate from the school. I knew I needed to take classes I was more passionate in and needed to make sure I have teachers I will be able to effectively communicate with.

My aspirations

My biggest goals for this class is to learn how to create great UIs and a website that can help me get a leg up on my competition when I am looking for a job. Both of which will help with my passions that would be working as a programmer with a fairly good wage and steady job. I want to be able to look at the website I created with pride knowing it helped me get to where I am at the time. I feel like presentation is extremely important in the hunt for jobs and one's life in general. Yet another thing I learned through programming through hackathons. You can have the most interesting code but if it's affects aren't that obvious it'll be passed over. Hopefully this class will help me achieve my dreams and help me give back to the greatest city and greatest college on earth.
